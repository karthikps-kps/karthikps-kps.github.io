Sales Statistics

Hypothesis testing for marketing activities.

statistical analysis

The aim of this project is to conduct hypothesis testing on sales statistics to provide insights on the performance of the sales before and after digital marketing was implemented, as well as determine whether there is any dependency between the features “Region” and “Manager”."

The project consists of two hypotheses to be tested:

1.

Null Hypothesis, H0 = "No Increase in Sales" After Stepping into Digital Marketing.
Alternate Hypothesis, HA = "Increase in Sales" After Stepping into Digital Marketing.

To test this hypothesis, a t-test for two samples is used, as there are two sets of data being compared. The t-test will help to determine whether the difference in means between the two sets of data is significant or not. Based on the results, if the p-value is less than 0.05, the null hypothesis will be rejected, indicating that there was an increase in sales after digital marketing was implemented. On the other hand, if the p-value is greater than 0.05, the null hypothesis will be accepted, indicating that there was no significant increase in sales after digital marketing was implemented.

2.

H0= There is no dependency between the features “Region” and “Manager”.
HA= There is a dependency between the features “Region” and “Manager”.

To test this hypothesis, the Chi-Square test of association is used. This test helps to determine whether there is a relationship between two categorical variables. The test calculates a chi-square statistic and a p-value to determine whether there is a significant association between the variables. Based on the results, if the p-value is less than 0.05, the null hypothesis will be rejected, indicating that there is a significant dependency between the features “Region” and “Manager”. On the other hand, if the p-value is greater than 0.05, the null hypothesis will be accepted, indicating that there is no significant dependency between the features “Region” and “Manager”.

The project uses Python and libraries such as numpy, pandas, matplotlib, seaborn, and scipy to perform the statistical analysis. The statistical values such as mean, standard deviation, and skewness are calculated to provide a better understanding of the data. Additionally, visualizations such as histograms, box plots, and scatter plots are used to gain insights into the distribution and relationship between the variables.

The project's conclusion will provide insights into the performance of sales before and after digital marketing was implemented, as well as whether there is a dependency between the features “Region” and “Manager”. These insights will be useful for the company to improve their sales strategies and make informed decisions.
