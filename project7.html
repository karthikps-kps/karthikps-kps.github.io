Demand Forecasting

Machine Learning model for Forecasting the Product Demand in a Retail Chain

Exploratory data analysis and ML model

In today's fast-paced retail industry, accurately predicting customer demand for products is crucial for effective inventory management and meeting customer needs. This project focuses on developing a forecasting system for a retail client to predict product demand based on past data. The objective is to provide accurate predictions that aid in inventory management and ensure customer satisfaction.

The methodology used in this project involved acquiring the dataset from Kaggle, which contains information on the sales of products across retail stores. Data analysis was conducted, including data preprocessing, categorizing the data based on product categories, analyzing each category separately, and checking for seasonality. Stationarity tests were performed, and non-stationary series were converted into stationary through differencing. Autocorrelation and partial autocorrelation plots were utilized to determine the parameters for the ARIMA model, and the auto ARIMA function was employed to choose the best set of parameters. The model was then fitted, and future sales values were predicted.

Some assumptions were made for this project, assuming the stability of the data from 2012 to 2014 without any hidden anomalies. The findings and analysis were presented through charts, tables, and diagrams, which provided insights into sales distribution, mean daily sales, and mean monthly sales.

In conclusion, demand forecasting plays a vital role in the retail industry. By leveraging time series models and analyzing historical sales data, accurate predictions can be made to optimize inventory management and meet customer expectations. This project serves as a valuable example of how data analysis and forecasting techniques can drive business success in the retail sector.
